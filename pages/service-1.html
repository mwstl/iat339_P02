Linear Spaces

Jakarta Indonesia 2022 Jul06

Experimental

SUMMARY

CLIENT REQUEST

Our client contacted us with the idea of creating a space where people's movements and experiences were linear. Everything could be expected, but nothing could be predicted.

OUR TASK

We began to scout out the location and identify the core values that the client wanted to express. The client focuses on art exhibits, and hopes to allow the venue to be utilized in perpetuity.

PROBLEM-SOLUTION

PROBLEM
  1. GEOGRAPHY

    The land is located on a slight incline.

  2. CLIMATE

    The area is prone to hot and humid weather.

  3. LIFESTYLE

    People travel, walk, bike, commute, and often crowd the streets throughout the day.

SOLUTION
  1. GEOGRAPHY

    The building was designed rely on this incline to create an underlying sense of imbalance, while maintaining safety protocols that would not endanger any visitor.

  2. CLIMATE

    Materials were chosen with the capability to keep the inhabitants cool and prevent mold and moisture build up.

  3. LIFESTYLE

    Major intersections and traffic areas were taken into account while designing the faces and facades of the building. Certain angles and windowed areas were avoided to prevent angling towards pedestrians throughout the seasons.

PROCESS

RESEARCH

We started by analyzing the area, land, weather, and concepts that the client initially gave to us. Along with information from the city and local cultures, we took into account pre-existing structures as a source of inspiration for our design.

IDEATION

Next up was to communicate our findings with the client and begin creating our initial concepts. The client was happy with our sloping design which included elements of Indonesia's culture and history.

REFINING

Then, through repeat feedback, we continually refined our designs to accomodate client requests while maintaining the core values identified earlier.
